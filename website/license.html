License & FAQ

FlamKit is licensed under the GNU General Public License v3 (GPLv3). Here's exactly what that means for you — in plain language.

The short version: The music you make with FlamKit is 100% yours — sell it, license it, do anything you like. Copyleft only applies if you modify and redistribute the plugin software itself.

Can I use FlamKit to make music I sell?
Yes. The music you produce with FlamKit is yours and is not a derivative work of the plugin. GPLv3 does not touch your tracks, your stems, or your releases. Sell them, stream them, license them for sync — no obligations to us, no royalties, nothing. (This is the single most important and most-misunderstood point, so we lead with it.)
Is it really free?
Yes — free to download and use, source available, no paid tiers, no sign-up, no trial that expires. Free forever is the whole point.
What does GPLv3 / copyleft actually mean for me?
If you modify and distribute FlamKit — the software — you must release your changes under GPLv3 and share your source code. That keeps the ecosystem open. Using the plugin to make music does not trigger this in any way. The vast majority of users simply download, install, and make beats — copyleft never enters the picture.
Can I build a closed-source product on top of FlamKit's code?
No. Copyleft requires derivative software to remain GPLv3 and open. You're free to fork FlamKit, build on it, and ship it — but the result must also be open-source under GPLv3. We're being upfront about this: we never describe FlamKit as "free for commercial use" without this caveat.
Why GPLv3?
Because it makes FlamKit community-governed and keeps it open. Contributions stay open, no one can take the project closed-source, and the tools you rely on can't be taken away from you. GPLv3 is a trust commitment, not a limitation.
What about the drum samples and kits?
The official starter kits are CC0 (public domain) — no attribution required, no restrictions, free for any use including commercial. Each kit lists its license and provenance on the Samples page.

This FAQ is plain-language guidance, not legal advice. For specific commercial or licensing questions, consult a lawyer.
